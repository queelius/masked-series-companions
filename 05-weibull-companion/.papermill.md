### Score Equations in maskedcauses (transcription targets)

The maskedcauses source already contains closed-form expressions that can be transcribed into mathematical notation:

- **Homogeneous Weibull, exact/right-censored**: closed-form analytical score (shape score involves `(t/beta_j)^k * log(t/beta_j)` terms; scale score involves hazard ratio `h_j(t)/h_sys(t)`)
- **Homogeneous Weibull, left/interval-censored**: log-likelihood is closed-form (`log(-expm1(...))` terms), but score uses `numDeriv::grad` — i.e., numerical gradient of an analytical log-likelihood
- **Heterogeneous Weibull**: all score computation is numerical

**Honesty requirement**: the paper must clearly distinguish closed-form analytical scores (exact/right) from numerical gradients of analytical log-likelihoods (left/interval). Do not present "explicit score equations" without this qualification.

### Pre-drafting priority: Resolve Result 4

Before writing any LaTeX, compute the FIM for homogeneous Weibull in the exact-observation, no-masking case. Key integral: E[log T] for Weibull T ~ Wei(k, beta) evaluates to `log(beta) + psi(1)/k` where psi is the digamma function. If FIM entries reduce to digamma/trigamma expressions, this is a clean headline result. If not, the paper's main theorem shifts to the numerical FIM characterization.
