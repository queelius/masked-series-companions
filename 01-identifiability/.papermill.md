## Partial Identifiability Caveat

The claim "each connected component of G corresponds to a super-component whose internal allocation is unresolvable" is **not universally true**. For heterogeneous Weibull with distinct shapes, linearly independent hazard functions h_j(t) = (k_j/beta_j)(t/beta_j)^{k_j-1} can restore identifiability even when components are diagnostically confounded (always co-occur). The power-function argument: distinct exponents k_j produce linearly independent functions of t.

**Resolution**: restrict the partial identifiability claims to exponential (constant hazards → confounded pairs truly lose one degree of freedom). Treat non-exponential identifiability-restoration as an open question, or attempt a proof for the Weibull case separately.

## Title Caveat

The current title "Identifiability and Information Loss in Masked Series Systems" implies generality across distribution families. If the concrete results stay exponential-centric, consider:
- "Identifiability and Information Loss in Masked Exponential Series Systems"
- "From Structural to Quantitative Identifiability in Masked Series Systems"
